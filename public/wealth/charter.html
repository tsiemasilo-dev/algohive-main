ALGOHIVE GLOBAL OPPORTUNITIES FUND GOVERNING CHARTER
SECTION 1 — DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION
1.1 Definitions
In this Charter, unless the context otherwise requires, the following terms shall have the meanings assigned to them below:
  • “AlgoHive” means AlgoHive (Pty) Ltd, registration number [2024/644796/07], the General Partner of the Fund.
  • “AlgoHive Global Opportunities Fund” or “the Fund” means the investment partnership governed by this Charter, established under South African law as an en commandite partnership.
  • “Charter” means this governing charter of the AlgoHive Global Opportunities Fund, as amended from time to time.
  • “General Partner” means AlgoHive, acting in its capacity as manager and administrator of the Fund.
  • “Limited Partner” means any person or entity admitted to the Fund in accordance with this Charter, other than the General Partner, including AlgoHive to the extent it contributes capital as a limited partner.
  • “Partnership” means the AlgoHive Global Opportunities Fund as constituted by the partnership agreement between the General Partner and Limited Partners.
  • “Partnership Agreement” means the formal agreement setting out the respective rights and obligations of the General Partner and the Limited Partners in accordance with this Charter.
  • “Contributor” means any person or entity subscribing for units or partnership interests in the Fund.
  • “Capital Contribution” means the amount invested by a Limited Partner into the Fund.
  • “Units” means the measure of a Limited Partner’s proportionate ownership interest in the Fund.
  • “Net Asset Value (NAV)” means the fair market value of the Fund’s assets less its liabilities, as determined in accordance with this Charter.
  • “Management Fee” means the 2% per annum fee payable to the General Partner for managing the Fund.
  • “Performance Fee” means the 20% of net profits allocated to the General Partner.
  • “Mirror-Investment Revenue Share” means the 80% of investment profits allocated to the Fund from its listed strategy on the AlgoHive platform.
  • “Financial Year” means the 12-month period ending 31 March of each year.
  • “Minimum Investment” means either a once-off contribution of R10,000 or a recurring monthly contribution of R1,000.
  • “South African Rand (R)” means the lawful currency of the Republic of South Africa.
1.2 Interpretation
  • Words importing the singular include the plural and vice versa.
  • References to persons include natural and juristic persons.
  • Headings are for convenience only and do not affect the interpretation of this Charter.
  • If any conflict arises between this Charter and the Partnership Agreement, the Partnership Agreement shall prevail to the extent of the inconsistency.
SECTION 2 — PURPOSE AND INVESTMENT OBJECTIVES
2.1 Purpose of the Fund

The AlgoHive Global Opportunities Fund is established to pool and manage the capital of its Limited Partners with the aim of delivering long-term, risk-adjusted returns through investments in high-quality businesses with sustainable competitive advantages, commonly referred to as “economic moats.” The Fund will apply a disciplined blend of fundamental and quantitative analysis to identify opportunities, consistent with the principles described in this Charter and the Partnership Agreement.

2.2 Investment Objective

The primary investment objective of the Fund is to achieve capital appreciation for Limited Partners through exposure to:

  • listed equities of companies with durable business models and strong market positions;
  • other financial instruments consistent with the Fund’s risk appetite and permitted by this Charter; and
  • where appropriate, cash or near-cash instruments for liquidity or risk management purposes.
2.3 Strategy

The Fund will pursue a moat-based equity strategy, supported by quantitative screening techniques and fundamental research to:

  • identify companies with structural competitive advantages;
  • assess pricing power, barriers to entry, and brand strength;
  • analyse consistent free cash flow generation; and
  • monitor valuation metrics to preserve a margin of safety.
2.4 Fund Management

The Fund shall be actively managed by the General Partner (AlgoHive), which will make investment decisions in accordance with the mandate of this Charter, exercising discretion to adjust the portfolio to respond to changing market conditions and opportunities, while respecting the principles of diversification and risk controls set out herein.

2.5 Investment Guidelines and Restrictions

The Fund will observe the following investment restrictions:

  • it shall not invest in unlisted securities except where approved by the General Partner and consistent with the Fund’s objectives;
  • it may use derivatives for risk management or portfolio hedging, but not for speculative leverage;
  • it will maintain sufficient diversification across sectors and geographies to limit concentration risk, as reasonably determined by the General Partner;
  • it will comply with applicable laws and regulations in South Africa and any other jurisdictions where it may operate.
2.6 Alignment with AlgoHive

The AlgoHive Moat Fund may be listed as a strategy on the AlgoHive investment platform. The Fund will retain 80% of profits generated from such a listing, while AlgoHive will earn 20% of those profits for listing and distribution of the strategy on its platform. Additionally, AlgoHive will receive transaction fees associated with third-party use of the strategy as described in Section 1.

2.7 Contribution by the General Partner

AlgoHive, in its capacity as General Partner, may contribute its own capital to the Fund and will participate in profit allocation as a Limited Partner to the extent of such contributions, subject to the same terms and conditions applicable to all Limited Partners, except where expressly set out otherwise in this Charter.

2.8 Commitment to Best Practices

The Fund shall be operated in line with industry best practices for risk management, transparency, and governance, including periodic independent audit of its financial statements, investor reporting on a quarterly basis, and strict adherence to anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) compliance processes.

SECTION 3 — STRUCTURE AND LEGAL STATUS
3.1 Legal Form

The AlgoHive Global Opportunities Fund is constituted as an en commandite partnership under the laws of the Republic of South Africa. The General Partner shall be AlgoHive (Pty) Ltd, registration number [2024/644796/07], with its principal place of business at [3 Gwen lane, Sandown, Sandton, Gauteng, South Africa].

3.2 General Partner

AlgoHive, in its capacity as General Partner, shall:

  • have the full authority and discretion to manage, administer, and represent the Fund in all matters;
  • act as agent and fiduciary for the Fund in its dealings with third parties;
  • be empowered to open and operate bank accounts in the name of the Fund, including ring-fenced accounts titled “AlgoHive Global Opportunities Fund Partnership Account” under AlgoHive’s banking arrangements, which shall be segregated from AlgoHive’s own operating accounts;
  • act at all times in good faith and in the best interests of the Fund and its Limited Partners.
3.3 Limited Partners

Limited Partners shall:

  • be admitted in accordance with the Partnership Agreement;
  • have no authority to bind the Fund or the General Partner;
  • bear no liability beyond their committed Capital Contribution except as required under applicable law;
  • share proportionately in the profits and losses of the Fund according to their respective Units, as recorded in the Fund’s register of interests.
3.4 AlgoHive as Limited Partner

AlgoHive may, in addition to its role as General Partner, contribute capital as a Limited Partner and shall be entitled to share in the profits of the Fund in the same manner as other Limited Partners in respect of its contributed capital, subject to any express exclusions of liability applicable to its General Partner role.

3.5 Fund Bank Accounts

All monies contributed to the Fund shall be deposited in bank accounts designated for the AlgoHive Global Opportunities Fund Partnership, separate from the general bank accounts of AlgoHive. The General Partner shall ensure that proper books and records are maintained in respect of the Fund’s accounts, investments, receipts, and distributions.

3.6 Rights of Limited Partners

Each Limited Partner shall have the following rights:

  • to receive statements of account and other reporting in accordance with this Charter;
  • to request information concerning their investment subject to reasonable notice and confidentiality safeguards;
  • to participate proportionately in profits, losses, and distributions;
  • to withdraw capital subject to the withdrawal provisions in Section 8;
  • to inspect, at reasonable times and on reasonable notice, the Fund’s records relating to their investment.
3.7 Term

The Fund shall have an initial term of [for example: 10 years, unless dissolved earlier], with rolling extensions at the General Partner’s discretion, unless otherwise terminated or liquidated under the terms of this Charter.

3.8 Regulatory Compliance

The Fund and its General Partner shall comply with all applicable regulatory requirements, including, but not limited to, any licensing, reporting, tax, anti-money laundering, and investor protection obligations under South African law and any other jurisdiction where the Fund operates.

SECTION 4 — FUND CONTRIBUTIONS AND UNITS
4.1 Capital Contributions

(a) Each Limited Partner shall contribute capital to the Fund in accordance with the minimum investment terms set out in this Charter, being a once-off contribution of not less than R10,000 or recurring monthly contributions of at least R1,000.

(b) The General Partner may, at its sole discretion, accept contributions exceeding these minimums, or waive the minimum contribution requirements in appropriate circumstances.

(c) Unless otherwise agreed by the General Partner, contributions shall be made in cash.

4.2 Units of Participation

(a) Each Limited Partner’s interest in the Fund shall be represented by Units, which will determine their proportional rights to the Fund’s profits, losses, and distributions.

(b) Units shall be allocated to each Limited Partner upon acceptance of their contribution, based on the prevailing Net Asset Value per Unit on the date of contribution.

(c) The General Partner shall maintain a register of Units, tracking each Limited Partner’s contributions, unit allocations, and any subsequent adjustments from withdrawals or additional subscriptions.

4.3 AlgoHive’s Contributions

(a) AlgoHive, as General Partner, may also make capital contributions to the Fund in its capacity as a Limited Partner. Such contributions shall be allocated Units on the same basis as other Limited Partners, subject to any separate management rights set out under this Charter.

4.4 Additional Fundraising

(a) The General Partner is authorised to raise additional capital for the Fund at its sole discretion, including from new Limited Partners, without requiring the consent or prior notification of existing Limited Partners, provided such fundraising complies with applicable law, regulatory guidelines, and the Partnership Agreement.

(b) Any new Limited Partner admitted will be subject to the same proportionate treatment of returns, risks, and obligations as existing Limited Partners, in accordance with this Charter.

4.5 Default in Contributions

(a) If a Limited Partner fails to meet a committed contribution, the General Partner may, at its discretion and without prejudice to other remedies:

(i) grant an extension to remedy the default;

(ii) apply reasonable penalties, including forfeiture of returns on the unpaid portion;

(iii) adjust or cancel that Limited Partner’s Units proportionately; or

(iv) take any other lawful step to protect the interests of the Fund and other Limited Partners.

4.6 Use of Capital Contributions

Capital contributions shall be used exclusively to implement the Fund’s investment strategy and cover its permitted costs, including but not limited to management fees, performance fees, transaction costs, compliance, audit and legal fees, and other reasonable expenses incurred in operating the Fund in accordance with best practice.

4.7 Evidence of Contribution

Each Limited Partner shall receive confirmation of their contribution, showing the Units issued, contribution amount, and the date of admission to the Fund.

SECTION 5 — MANAGEMENT AND GOVERNANCE
5.1 Management Authority

(a) The General Partner shall have full discretion and authority to manage and administer the Fund, including making all investment and operational decisions necessary to achieve the objectives set out in this Charter.

(b) The General Partner may delegate functions to agents, advisers, or other service providers as it deems appropriate, provided that overall responsibility and fiduciary oversight remain with the General Partner.

(c) The General Partner shall act at all times in good faith and in a manner consistent with the best interests of the Fund and its Limited Partners.

5.2 Decision-Making Powers

(a) Except where explicitly stated otherwise in this Charter or required by law, the General Partner shall have absolute authority over the Fund’s activities, including:

(i) investment selection and execution;

(ii) cash management and bank account operation;

(iii) acceptance of new Limited Partners and fundraising decisions;

(iv) contracting with brokers, custodians, administrators, or other third parties;

(v) setting operational policies to safeguard Fund assets.

(b) Limited Partners shall have no role in the day-to-day or investment decision-making of the Fund and may not bind the Fund or the General Partner in any manner.

5.3 Fees and Compensation

(a) The Fund shall pay the General Partner a management fee of 2% per annum of the Fund’s Net Asset Value, calculated and accrued monthly.

(b) The Fund shall pay the General Partner a performance fee of 20% of the net profits earned by the Fund, calculated annually and paid within 30 days of the Fund’s financial year-end.

(c) Where the Fund’s strategy is listed on the AlgoHive platform and mirrored by third parties, AlgoHive shall receive 20% of any mirror-investment profits, with the Fund retaining 80% of those mirror-investment profits.

(d) The General Partner is further entitled to transaction fees generated through the AlgoHive platform associated with the Fund’s listed strategy.

5.4 Conflicts of Interest

(a) The General Partner shall disclose to Limited Partners any material conflicts of interest which, in its reasonable opinion, may significantly and adversely affect the Fund.

(b) The General Partner may manage other funds, vehicles, or accounts with strategies that are similar to or overlap with the Fund, provided it acts in good faith to allocate opportunities fairly and in a manner consistent with applicable law.

(c) Limited Partners acknowledge and accept the possibility of such conflicts and agree that the General Partner’s duty of good faith and fair dealing shall be sufficient protection in these circumstances.

5.5 Reporting and Oversight

(a) The General Partner shall provide Limited Partners with quarterly performance statements, including valuations, fees charged, and portfolio holdings summaries, subject to reasonable confidentiality limitations.

(b) The Fund’s annual financial statements shall be independently reviewed or audited by a qualified external auditor, whose appointment shall be at the General Partner’s discretion.

(c) The General Partner shall maintain adequate systems, controls, and policies to comply with anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorist financing (CTF), and know-your-client (KYC) requirements as applicable under South African regulations.

SECTION 6 — INVESTMENT POLICIES
6.1 Eligible Investments

The Fund shall invest primarily in listed equities of companies demonstrating sustainable competitive advantages, commonly described as “economic moats.” The Fund may also, at the General Partner’s discretion, invest in:

(a) exchange-traded funds (ETFs) or other collective investment schemes with a similar moat-based or market exposure strategy;

(b) listed debt instruments or preference shares where these support the overall investment objective;

(c) cash or near-cash instruments for liquidity, risk management, or tactical allocation;

(d) derivatives for hedging or efficient portfolio management, but not for speculative leverage; and

(e) any other security or instrument permitted by applicable laws and consistent with this Charter.

(f) mirror investment strategies on AlgoHive platform that resemble its investment philosophy.

6.2 Investment Guidelines

(a) The General Partner shall manage the Fund in accordance with prudent investment principles, balancing risk and reward to achieve the stated objectives.

(b) The Fund shall maintain sufficient diversification by sector, issuer, and geography to avoid excessive concentration risk.

(c) The Fund may not invest more than 25% of its assets in a single issuer, except for cash or government securities, without the General Partner’s explicit documented risk assessment.

(d) The Fund may not invest more than 10% of its assets in unlisted securities without the approval of the General Partner and proper disclosure to Limited Partners.

6.3 Use of Derivatives

(a) Derivatives may be used solely for the purpose of hedging or efficient portfolio management.

(b) The Fund shall not use derivatives to establish speculative leveraged positions exceeding the Fund’s total committed capital.

(c) Counterparty exposures in derivative transactions shall be monitored to ensure prudent risk management in accordance with the Fund’s risk policy.

6.4 Borrowing and Leverage

(a) The Fund may borrow on a short-term basis for liquidity management or to meet redemption obligations, subject to a maximum leverage ratio of 20% of its Net Asset Value at any time.

(b) Longer-term leverage is permitted only where specifically approved by the General Partner, and subject to written risk assessments and compliance with applicable laws.

6.5 Risk Controls

(a) The General Partner shall establish and maintain risk monitoring procedures, including stress testing and scenario analysis, to protect the Fund’s assets.

(b) The Fund shall comply with all applicable risk management requirements under South African law and any other relevant jurisdiction.

(c) The General Partner shall document and periodically review investment risk policies to ensure they remain fit for purpose.

6.6 Responsible Investment

The Fund supports responsible investment practices. The General Partner shall consider material environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors as appropriate in its investment analysis, provided these do not compromise the Fund’s primary objective of delivering superior risk-adjusted returns.

SECTION 7 — FUND ACCOUNTING, VALUATION, AND REPORTING
7.1 Accounting Principles

The Fund’s accounts shall be maintained in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) applicable in South Africa, consistently applied/IFRS, whichever Is chosen and deemed appropriate. All financial transactions, including capital contributions, investment purchases and sales, income, expenses, fees, and distributions, shall be accurately recorded and supported by appropriate documentation.

7.2 Valuation of Assets

(a) The Net Asset Value (NAV) of the Fund shall be calculated at least monthly, using fair market values for all assets held by the Fund.

(b) Listed securities shall be valued at the closing price on the relevant stock exchange as of the valuation date.

(c) Unlisted securities shall be valued at fair value as determined by the General Partner using recognized valuation methodologies, including discounted cash flow, comparable transactions, or third-party appraisals where appropriate.

(d) Cash and cash equivalents shall be valued at face value.

(e) Any material valuation adjustments shall be documented and disclosed to Limited Partners in periodic reports.

7.3 Unit Pricing and Allocations

(a) The NAV per Unit shall be calculated by dividing the Fund’s NAV by the total number of Units outstanding at the valuation date.

(b) Contributions and withdrawals shall be processed at the NAV per Unit on the relevant effective date.

(c) Profits, losses, fees, and distributions shall be allocated among Limited Partners in proportion to their Units.

7.4 Financial Reporting to Limited Partners

(a) The General Partner shall provide Limited Partners with quarterly statements including:

  • the Fund’s NAV and NAV per Unit;
  • portfolio holdings summary;
  • fees charged and expenses incurred;
  • any material events or disclosures.

(b) Annual audited or reviewed financial statements shall be provided within 120 days after the financial year-end, prepared by an independent auditor appointed by the General Partner.

7.5 Record Keeping

The General Partner shall maintain accurate and complete records of all Fund activities, including investor registers, accounting records, correspondence, contracts, and compliance documentation, for a minimum period of seven (7) years.

7.6 Access to Information

Limited Partners may, upon reasonable notice and during normal business hours, inspect the Fund’s records relating to their investment, subject to confidentiality agreements to protect the Fund’s proprietary information and other Limited Partners’ privacy.

SECTION 8 — WITHDRAWALS, REDEMPTIONS, AND TRANSFERS
8.1 Withdrawals by Limited Partners

(a) Limited Partners may request withdrawals of their invested capital on a quarterly basis, subject to providing at least 30 calendar days’ prior written notice to the General Partner.

(b) The General Partner may, at its discretion, accept withdrawal requests on shorter notice in exceptional cases.

(c) Withdrawal payments shall be made at the NAV per Unit calculated as of the end of the relevant quarter.

(d) The General Partner may limit or suspend withdrawals if it reasonably determines that satisfying such requests would materially harm the Fund’s liquidity, portfolio balance, or other Limited Partners.

8.2 Withdrawal Limits and Gatekeeping

(a) The General Partner may impose a withdrawal cap (“gate”) on any redemption period, limiting total withdrawals to 20% of the Fund’s NAV per quarter, to protect the Fund’s orderly management.

(b) If total withdrawal requests exceed this gate, withdrawals shall be processed on a pro-rata basis, and deferred amounts will automatically roll forward to the next available redemption window.

8.3 Suspension of Withdrawals

The General Partner may suspend withdrawals temporarily under the following circumstances:

(a) if there is a material disruption in the markets affecting valuation of the Fund’s assets;

(b) where there is a legal or regulatory restriction preventing transactions;

(c) in the event of natural disaster, war, or other force majeure;

(d) if such suspension is in the reasonable opinion of the General Partner necessary to protect the interests of the Fund and its Limited Partners.

8.4 Transfers of Units

(a) Limited Partners may not transfer, assign, pledge, or otherwise dispose of their Units without the prior written consent of the General Partner, which may be withheld at its discretion.

(b) The General Partner shall have the right of first refusal to purchase any Units proposed for transfer on the same terms as offered to any third party.

(c) Any permitted transferee must meet all applicable legal, regulatory, and AML/KYC requirements before being recorded as a Limited Partner.

8.5 Compulsory Withdrawals

The General Partner may compulsorily withdraw all or part of a Limited Partner’s interest if:

(a) required by law or regulation;

(b) necessary to protect the Fund’s regulatory or tax status;

(c) the Limited Partner has breached this Charter or the Partnership Agreement;

(d) the Limited Partner’s continued participation would, in the reasonable opinion of the General Partner, prejudice the Fund or its other Limited Partners.

SECTION 9 — DISTRIBUTIONS AND ALLOCATIONS
9.1 Allocation of Profits and Losses

(a) Profits and losses of the Fund shall be allocated to Limited Partners in proportion to their Units as recorded in the Fund’s register, after deduction of management fees, performance fees, expenses, and any reserves the General Partner deems necessary.

(b) AlgoHive, as General Partner, shall share in allocations in respect of any capital it has contributed to the Fund as a Limited Partner, on the same terms as other Limited Partners.

9.2 Distribution Policy

(a) Distributions of available cash profits, if any, shall generally be made annually, unless the General Partner determines it is in the best interests of the Fund to retain profits for reinvestment.

(b) Distributions shall be made only after the Fund has satisfied its liabilities, reserves, and reasonable working capital needs.

(c) All distributions shall be made in proportion to each Limited Partner’s Units.

9.3 Reinvestment of Profits

Limited Partners may elect in writing to automatically reinvest their distributions into the Fund, subject to acceptance by the General Partner and subject to prevailing NAV per Unit as at the reinvestment date.

9.4 Priority of Payments

Distributable amounts shall be paid in the following order of priority:

(a) payment of any due Fund liabilities;

(b) reserves for expenses and contingencies as determined by the General Partner;

(c) management fees and performance fees due to the General Partner;

(d) distribution to Limited Partners in proportion to their Units.

9.5 Withholding Taxes

If required by law, the General Partner shall withhold and remit any applicable taxes from distributions and provide supporting tax documentation to Limited Partners as reasonably required.

SECTION 10 — CONFLICTS OF INTEREST AND RELATED-PARTY TRANSACTIONS
10.1 General Standard

The General Partner shall manage the Fund honestly, fairly, and in good faith, and in a manner that prioritises the interests of the Fund and its Limited Partners above its own interests, except to the extent disclosed and permitted under this Charter.

10.2 Conflicts of Interest

(a) The General Partner may act as manager or advisor to other funds, partnerships, or investment accounts with similar strategies. Limited Partners acknowledge and accept the possibility of such parallel activities, provided the General Partner uses reasonable care to allocate investment opportunities fairly.

(b) The General Partner must disclose to Limited Partners any material conflict of interest that, in its reasonable opinion, could significantly and adversely affect the Fund.

(c) Where conflicts arise, the General Partner shall adopt conflict resolution policies consistent with industry best practice and regulatory requirements.

10.3 Related-Party Transactions

(a) The Fund may enter into transactions with the General Partner or its affiliates, provided that such transactions are on terms no less favourable to the Fund than those available from unrelated third parties on an arm’s-length basis.

(b) Any related-party transaction involving amounts exceeding 5% of the Fund’s NAV must be documented with a written rationale and disclosed to Limited Partners in the next quarterly report.

(c) The Fund shall keep records of all related-party transactions for inspection by Limited Partners on reasonable notice.

10.4 Dealings with AlgoHive Platform

(a) Limited Partners acknowledge that the Fund’s investment strategy may be listed on the AlgoHive platform and mirrored by third-party clients. The Fund will retain 80% of profits from any mirror-investment profits, while AlgoHive will receive 20% of such profits, as described in Section 5.

(b) Any listing, promotion, or transaction through the AlgoHive platform shall be considered a permitted related-party arrangement under this Charter.

10.5 Disclosures

(a) The General Partner shall maintain a register of all material conflicts of interest and make this available to Limited Partners on request, subject to reasonable confidentiality protections.

(b) The General Partner shall review conflict-of-interest policies at least annually to ensure ongoing compliance with legal and ethical standards.

SECTION 11 — REGULATORY AND COMPLIANCE MATTERS
11.1 Regulatory Framework

The Fund shall operate under all applicable laws and regulations of the Republic of South Africa, including the Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act (FAIS), the Collective Investment Schemes Control Act (CISCA) to the extent applicable, and any other relevant legislation, as amended from time to time.

11.2 FSCA Licensing

(a) The General Partner shall ensure that any required licences or approvals from the Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) are obtained and maintained, including any required Category II discretionary mandates.

(b) The Fund shall comply with all relevant codes of conduct, fit-and-proper requirements, and client protection rules prescribed by the FSCA.

11.3 Anti-Money Laundering and KYC

(a) The Fund shall implement and maintain robust anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorist financing (CTF), and know-your-customer (KYC) policies, in accordance with the Financial Intelligence Centre Act (FICA) and any other relevant legislation.

(b) Limited Partners shall provide the necessary identification documents and other information to comply with these obligations before being admitted to the Fund.

11.4 Tax Compliance

(a) The General Partner shall ensure the Fund complies with all tax laws, including registration for any relevant tax numbers and preparation of tax returns.

(b) The Fund shall comply with international tax transparency initiatives, including the Common Reporting Standard (CRS) and the Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (FATCA), where applicable.

(c) Limited Partners may be required to provide tax residency declarations and other relevant tax information to allow the Fund to meet its reporting obligations.

11.5 Cross-Border Requirements

(a) The Fund may accept investments from foreign Limited Partners, provided such acceptance does not breach any South African or international law, including exchange control regulations.

(b) The General Partner shall ensure that any cross-border marketing, promotion, or acceptance of funds complies with the requirements of the South African Reserve Bank (SARB) and other relevant authorities.

11.6 Dispute Resolution

(a) The Fund shall include a dispute resolution mechanism in its Partnership Agreement, providing for mediation or arbitration under the Arbitration Foundation of Southern Africa rules as the preferred forum for resolving disputes.

(b) Nothing in this Charter prevents either party from approaching a court with jurisdiction if required by law.

11.7 Reporting to Regulators

The General Partner shall prepare and submit all required reports to the FSCA, SARS, the FIC, or other competent regulators, in the form and frequency required by applicable legislation and best practice standards.

SECTION 12 — TERMINATION AND DISSOLUTION
12.1 Term of the Fund

(a) The Fund shall have an initial term of ten (10) years from its formation date, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Charter.

(b) The General Partner may extend the term of the Fund in successive one-year periods after the initial term, unless a majority (by value) of Limited Partners object in writing within 60 days of notice of extension.

12.2 Early Termination Events

The Fund may be terminated before expiry of its term upon:

(a) a written decision of the General Partner, provided reasonable notice is given to Limited Partners;

(b) unanimous consent of all Limited Partners;

(c) a material breach of applicable law, regulation, or fiduciary duty making continuation unlawful or impossible, as certified by competent legal opinion;

(d) an order by a competent court or regulator to wind up the Fund.

12.3 Dissolution Procedure

(a) Upon termination of the Fund, the General Partner shall proceed with an orderly liquidation of the Fund’s assets, having regard to maximising fair value for Limited Partners.

(b) Fund liabilities shall be settled first from the available assets, followed by repayment of Limited Partner contributions, and finally distribution of any remaining profits in accordance with their proportionate Units.

(c) The General Partner shall provide a final liquidation report to all Limited Partners within 90 days of completing the dissolution.

12.4 Preservation of Records

The General Partner shall retain all books, records, and financial statements of the Fund for at least seven (7) years after dissolution, in accordance with applicable law.

12.5 Limitation of Claims

Except for fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence, no Limited Partner may pursue claims against the General Partner after one (1) year following the final liquidation report.

SECTION 13 — MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS
13.1 Governing Law

This Charter shall be governed by and interpreted in accordance with the laws of the Republic of South Africa.

13.2 Notices

(a) Any notice, demand, or other formal communication required under this Charter shall be made in writing and delivered by hand, courier, registered mail, or electronic mail to the addresses recorded for the Limited Partners in the Fund’s register, or to such other address as may be notified in writing.

(b) Notices shall be deemed received:

(i) if delivered by hand or courier, on the date of delivery;

(ii) if sent by registered mail, seven (7) days after posting;

(iii) if sent by email, on confirmation of successful transmission.

13.3 Amendments

(a) This Charter may be amended by the General Partner, provided any material amendment that adversely affects the rights of Limited Partners requires approval from Limited Partners holding at least a majority (by value) of Units.

(b) Non-material or regulatory amendments may be made unilaterally by the General Partner to maintain compliance with laws or best practices.

13.4 Severability

If any provision of this Charter is found to be invalid or unenforceable, that provision shall be interpreted to best reflect the parties’ original intent, and the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

13.5 Entire Agreement

This Charter, together with the Partnership Agreement and any other Fund governance documents, constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior oral or written agreements in respect of the subject matter.

13.6 Counterparts and Execution

This Charter may be signed in counterparts, including electronically, and all counterparts together shall constitute one binding agreement.
